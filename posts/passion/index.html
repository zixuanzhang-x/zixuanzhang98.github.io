What Does It Mean to Be a Passionate Engineer?

Zixuan Zhang
 2021-11-17  197 words   One minute 
Contents

Let’s Be Honest

Although every engineer claims to be passionate and lifelong learners, few of them really are.

What Is Passion?

What is passion? It’s not something we only talk about during interviews or presentations. Instead, it’s something we do, some choices we make, and some paths we take.

More specifically, it’s about:

  • still searching for a better solution even when you already have a suboptimal one that works for now.

  • being excited about new technologies and new challenges even if that means going into the unknown.

  • keeping trying even though you’re likely to fail or looks like a fool.

  • not getting upset about failure because you know failure is the acceptable norm.

  • taking the time to learn something you don’t have to know because your pure desire to understand more.

  • getting so excited about this cool technology you just learned that you can’t fell asleep.

  • thinking so hard about a problem that it fills out your dreams.

  • being responsible to your code and leaving as little technical debt as possible.

  • pursuing perfect solution to the extreme.

  • when you come to the crossroads in life, choose the right path. The easy path is just so darn boring.
